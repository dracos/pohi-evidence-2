CHIEF EXECUTIVE'S OFFICE

Dear Ms Vennels,

Is Post Office Ltd stealing my money?

I am a Subpostmaster at Dunston Post Office in Gateshead, fad no. 225 320
1 and I pose this question with all seriousness. I have been having a problem
with unexplained shortages for more than 2 years. I had put in place a system
of checks and double checks, so much so that we generally do not get any
transaction corrections.

The operation of the office was reviewed by a subpostmaster with some 30
years of experience to assess the performance and see any obvious
shortcomings. He did not discover any problems, but was not able to shed
any light on the unexplained shortages.

I had tried to get the Help line to help without success, I discussed these
problems and requested help from 3 different contracts managers over the
course of last 3 years to help me, but no help materialised.

With the unexplained losses increasing we started to do a cash balance twice
a day at lunchtime and in the evening, because my part time staff change
shifts at lunchtime. It did not stop the losses but at least captured them within
4 hours period and I know when they occurred.

To ensure that the money is not lost due to staff stealing, I had installed CCTV
system with two cameras on the counter with the staff agreement. After
spending countless hours viewing videos I can state that transaction were
carried out correctly and nobody took any money.

I attach a table of losses going back to August 2011 although I have much
records back to 2009 and from the distribution of the loses it is apparent that
the loses tend to occur in periods of heavy demand, particularly on Mondays.
We were settling all losses to central, mainly because I did not believe that
those losses were due to any mistakes made in the branch. Thus a
substantial backlog of ‘debt’ was accumulating in Chesterfield.

As I was not getting any help from Post Office Ltd. and the loses were not
sustainable, I decided by the end of 2011 to sell the post office. The sale was
going through and was to be completed on 3 May 12 but it was postponed by
POL,

Then on 2" of May our gateway computer ( 3 position office) failed completely
and we also lost ADSL connection. The ADSL was reinstated on 3 May by
replacing the ADSL hub. The gateway computer was replaced on 4 May 2012
at the same time as the audit team walked in.

The auditors have audited and ‘found out’ all the loses from Chesterfield and
whatever else was in the pipeline. I was suspended (for eight weeks), and
suspension was reversed once I gave undertaking to repay all these losses to
POL.

Once we reopened after the suspension was lifted, we served on the replaced
gateway computer. The remaining two computers had to be replaced within
24 hrs of opening due to a flash flood.

So now we are in the sixth week of trading with all equipment replaced and
we are balancing every day to a penny, we have not had one unexplained
loss since we re-started..

I know that you have a great faith in Horizon system which is working fine now
but we had serious problems before the equipment was replaced. I employ
the same staff who operate in the same manner and yet there are no loses
compared to daily loses for two/three years prior to equipment replacement.

I have been asking the contracts managers over last two to two and half years
for the equipment to be replaced as it seemed suspect but all those requests
were denied.

I hope that you will be able to satisfactorily explain why replacement of the
hardware has changed the situation so dramatically so that we are balancing
to a penny instead of losing thousands of pounds.

I believe that Post Office Ltd. has failed to maintain the equipment adequately
and it produced the loses. And if the loses were caused by the faulty
hardware, why would POL expect me to repay all the loses which was beyond
my powers to stop them. It was only POL who could replace the faulty
equipment.
