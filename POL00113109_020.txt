SUMMARY OF FACTS PREPARED IN ACCORDANCE WITH
THE CRIMNAL PROCEDURE RULES 2011

The defendant (DOB “I aged 34, was the sub postmaster at
the Cold Norton Post Office, Chelmsford. He had been in service
with Post Office Limited since June 2009. The office is a small sub
post office inside a convenience store also owned by the
defendant. A routine audit was conducted on the 31st May 2012,
which identified a short fall of cash and stock of £77905.02.

The defendant was present from the outset of the audit, when he
was asked to place all the cash and stock for audit on the counter,
and confirm that the safe was empty. However during the
subsequent procedure, the defendant introduced a further £5000 in
£20 notes, which he stated had been in the inner safe. This was
added to the cash for audit. Empty remittance pouches ( normally
used to send excess cash held at the office back to the cash centre)
to the value of £50000 were recovered. System analysis showed
another £15000 to have been sent but not received. Later during
interview the defendant admitted that these had been created and
scanned into the computerised accounting system to help create a
false account. Together with further discrepancy shortfalls in the
cash on hand, foreign currency and stock figures the total came to
£77905.02.

assigned to the case and invited the defendant for interview under
caution on the 6" June 2012.
