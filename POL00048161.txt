POST OFFICE LIMITED v JANET LOUISE SKINNER
CASE NO: POLTD/0607/0108

In my opinion the evidence is sufficient to afford a realistic prospect of
conviction of Janet Louise Skinner on the charge set out on the attached
schedule. There is a medium prospect of success. In my opinion the
charge is appropriate at this stage bearing in mind the claims made by the
Defendant and in consistent actions in concealing the loss.

In view of the charge of theft I would not advise charges of false accounting
at this stage.

In view of the serious nature of this offence the case is not in my opinion
suitable for a caution.

The case is in my opinion more suitable for trial in the Crown Court.

In the event that prosecution is authorised please obtain summonses.

The matters which follow are for the investigation manager to deal with.

No further statements need to be obtained at this stage.

Should the Defendant elect trial or if the Magistrates refuse jurisdiction or if a

not guilty plea is entered, the Investigation Manager should obtain all

necessary statements and exhibits. I list below those statements which

appear to be necessary in this case, but the investigation manager should

also consider whether there are any other areas which can reasonably be

covered and include any such evidence.

1 Copy statement explaining the Defendant’s contract of service with the
Post office dealing with how long she has been employed and at

which Offices.

2 Copy statement dealing with the accounts and explaining how the loss
arose.
