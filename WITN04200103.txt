CONTRACT 1 (the Authorities’ Agreement) DSS(1) POCL(2) ICL Pathway(3)

‘This contract is an umberella contract and deals with matters affecting both DSS and POCL
eg the joint operational trial and the charging mechanism.

ICL Pathway failed to complete operational trial under this agreement by the due date of 21
November 1997. They also failed to complete the operational trial by the end of the 13 week
cure notice period on 12 August 1998.

If failure to complete the operational trial is ICL. Pathway’s fault, the DSS is entitled to
terminate Contract 2 (the DSS Agreement) and doing so relieves the DSS of further rights
and obligations under Contract 1 (the Authorities’ Agreement). Similar: provisions apply to
POCL and Contract 3 (the POCL Agreement).

The Joint Programme Lawyer advises that there is sufficient evidence that the failure to
complete operational trial was ICL Pathway’s fault. This view is stipported by work
commissioned by him from Project Mentors, who are independant consultants.

It follows that the DSS is entitled to terminate Contract 2 (the DSS Agreement) with the
consequence that the DSS would have no further rights or obligations under Contract 1 (the
Authorities’ Agreement).
